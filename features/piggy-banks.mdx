## Overview

Piggy banks help you save money toward specific goals within your existing accounts. Whether saving for a vacation, emergency fund, or new purchase, piggy banks track your progress and motivate you to reach your targets.

## What Are Piggy Banks?

A piggy bank is a savings goal linked to one of your asset accounts:
- **Emergency Fund**: Save $5,000 in your savings account
- **Vacation Fund**: Set aside $2,000 for a trip
- **New Laptop**: Save $1,500 for an upgrade
- **House Down Payment**: Build $30,000 for a home

<Note>
Piggy banks don't hold actual money. They track a portion of money already in your accounts that's allocated for a specific purpose.
</Note>

## How Piggy Banks Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Piggy Bank">
    Set up a savings goal with a target amount.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Link to an Account">
    Connect it to an asset account (savings, checking, etc.).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Money">
    Transfer money to the piggy bank as you save.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track Progress">
    Watch your progress toward the goal.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Remove Money When Needed">
    Take money out when you use it for the goal.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Example: Vacation Piggy Bank">
  1. Create "Summer Vacation" piggy bank with $2,000 target
  2. Link to your savings account
  3. Add $200 each month when you get paid
  4. After 10 months, you have $2,000 saved
  5. Remove the $2,000 when you book the vacation
  6. Your savings account balance decreases as you spend on vacation
</Accordion>

## Creating a Piggy Bank

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Piggy Banks">
    Go to **Piggy Banks** in the main navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create New Piggy Bank">
    Click **Create New Piggy Bank**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Details">
    Fill in the information:
    - **Name**: Descriptive goal name ("Emergency Fund")
    - **Target Amount**: How much you want to save
    - **Account**: Which asset account holds this savings
    - **Currency**: The currency (usually matches the account)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Optional Details">
    - **Start Date**: When you begin saving
    - **Target Date**: Deadline for reaching the goal
    - **Notes**: Why you're saving or additional context
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to create your piggy bank.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Money to Piggy Banks

To save money toward a goal:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Piggy Bank">
    Click on the piggy bank from the list.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Add Money">
    Select **Add Money** or **+**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Amount">
    Type how much you're allocating to this goal.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Save** to record the addition.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Adding money to a piggy bank doesn't create a transaction or move money between accounts. It just tracks that a portion of your account balance is allocated for this goal.
</Warning>

## Removing Money from Piggy Banks

When you use saved money:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Piggy Bank">
    View the piggy bank details.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Remove Money">
    Select **Remove Money** or **-**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Amount">
    Type how much you're using.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Save** to record the removal.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Remove money from the piggy bank when you create the transaction that spends it. This keeps your savings tracking accurate.
</Tip>

## Managing Piggy Banks

### Editing Piggy Banks

To modify a piggy bank:
1. Open the piggy bank
2. Click **Edit**
3. Update any fields
4. Save changes

<Note>
Changing the target amount doesn't affect the current saved amount. Only adding/removing money changes the balance.
</Note>

### Deleting Piggy Banks

To delete a piggy bank:
1. Open the piggy bank details
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm deletion

<Warning>
Deleting a piggy bank removes all savings history. The money remains in your account, but the tracking is lost. Consider marking it inactive instead.
</Warning>

### Active vs. Inactive

- **Active piggy banks**: Show on dashboard and in lists
- **Inactive piggy banks**: Hidden but preserve history

Use inactive status for:
- Completed goals you want to track historically
- Temporarily paused savings goals
- Goals you might resume later

## Piggy Bank Strategies

### The Emergency Fund

Build financial security:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Target">
    Aim for 3-6 months of expenses (e.g., $10,000).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Regular Contributions">
    Add money each payday automatically.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Don't Remove Unless Emergency">
    Keep this money untouched for real emergencies.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Multiple Small Goals

Save for several things at once:
- Vacation: $2,000
- New Laptop: $1,500
- Holiday Gifts: $1,000

Contribute to each based on priority and timeline.

### Savings Categories

Create piggy banks for different purposes:
- **Short-term** (< 1 year): Vacation, gifts, repairs
- **Medium-term** (1-5 years): Car down payment, home improvement
- **Long-term** (5+ years): House down payment, retirement travel

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Set Realistic Goals">
  Make targets achievable based on your income and expenses. Overly ambitious goals lead to frustration. Start smaller and increase as you succeed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Use Target Dates Wisely">
  Set deadlines for goals with specific timing:
  - Summer vacation: Need money by June
  - Holiday gifts: Target by December 1
  - Tax payment: Due April 15
  
  No deadline needed for open-ended goals like emergency funds.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Keep Piggy Banks in Separate Savings Accounts">
  Link piggy banks to savings accounts, not your main checking account. This:
  - Reduces temptation to spend
  - Earns interest on savings
  - Provides clearer separation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Automate Contributions">
  Set up recurring transfers:
  1. Create automatic transfer to savings account
  2. Manually add to piggy bank(s) each payday
  3. Or use rules to auto-update piggy banks
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Review Progress Monthly">
  Check your piggy banks each month:
  - Are you on track?
  - Do targets need adjusting?
  - Should you increase contributions?
</Accordion>

## Common Use Cases

### Vacation Savings

- **Target**: Trip cost estimate
- **Timeline**: Travel date
- **Contribution**: Monthly until trip
- **Completion**: Remove money as you book and travel

### Emergency Fund

- **Target**: 3-6 months expenses
- **Timeline**: Open-ended (build over time)
- **Contribution**: Regular deposits
- **Completion**: Never "complete" - maintain this balance

### Large Purchase

- **Target**: Item cost
- **Timeline**: When you need/want it
- **Contribution**: Aggressive saving
- **Completion**: Remove all when making purchase

### Sinking Fund

- **Target**: Annual/irregular expense (insurance, taxes)
- **Timeline**: Due date
- **Contribution**: Divide total by months until due
- **Completion**: Remove when paying expense

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Piggy bank balance seems wrong">
  Check:
  - Review event history for unexpected changes
  - Verify you removed money when spending it
  - Confirm you added money for each contribution
  - Look for duplicate additions
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can't add more money than account balance">
  Remember: Piggy banks track money already in your account. If your savings account has $1,000, you can't allocate $1,500 to piggy banks linked to that account. Transfer more money to the account first.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Progress not showing correctly">
  Possible issues:
  - Target amount changed without adjusting saved amount
  - Currency mismatch between piggy bank and account
  - Browser cache (try refreshing)
</Accordion>
